## 1. Market Overview

### Size of the US Insurance Call Center / BPO Market

Multiple analyst sources paint a clear picture of a large and growing market:

**Best estimate for US insurance call center/BPO spend:** ~$8-12B annually in the US (North America ~35% of global = ~$22B of broader market). The segment Glima is targeting ‚Äî structured voice call automation for carriers and MGAs ‚Äî represents a subset of this, estimated at **$3-5B in addressable US spend**.

### How Much Is Outsourced vs. In-House

- **Majority of mid-size and small carriers outsource FNOL and claims-adjacent call center operations** to specialists. Large carriers (State Farm, Progressive, Allstate) run captive call centers.
- Industry surveys indicate **60-70% of carriers use some form of outsourcing** for at least one call center function.
- FNOL in particular is "more frequently outsourced to a specialist" than handled in-house (ACTEC).
- P&C lines captured **44.5% of the insurance BPO market** in 2024 due to high transaction volume and multi-party claims complexity (Mordor Intelligence).
- **Life and pension lead** overall BPO market share at 58.9% (IMARC) ‚Äî but these are largely back-office processes, not call center.

### Trends Driving the Market

**1. Labor Cost Surge**
Post-COVID labor markets pushed US call center agent wages to **$18-25/hour for standard agents** and **$30-50/hour for licensed insurance agents** (Expivia). Outsourcing to traditional BPO saves 30-40% vs. in-house; AI automation can deliver **40-60% savings vs. traditional BPO** (Sonant.ai).

**2. AI Disruption ‚Äî Now**
Voice AI has crossed the quality threshold. ElevenLabs' conversational agents (SOC2/GDPR/HIPAA compliant, 30+ languages) are being deployed in production customer service contexts. ElevenLabs launched its AIUC-1 ("AI Underwriter Certification") to enable enterprise insurance deployment at scale (Feb 2026). Floatbot's LISA/ADDI agents claim **80% automation of FNOL submissions**. Liberate handles **75% of digital FNOL with minimal carrier involvement**.

**3. Offshoring vs. Onshoring Tension**
There is a strong trend back toward US-based ("onshore") outsourcing, especially post-COVID quality complaints about offshore centers. Covenir has built an entire brand around "100% US-based onshore outsourcing." This creates an opening for AI ‚Äî which is inherently onshore, 24/7, and accent-neutral.

**4. Carrier Consolidation Creating Small-Carrier Vulnerability**
The top 5 auto insurers captured 63.59% of the market in 2024 (S&P Global). Small and regional carriers are being squeezed on cost ‚Äî making them prime buyers of cost-saving AI call center solutions.

**5. Why Now Is Compelling**
- Voice AI quality is now indistinguishable from human for structured interactions
- Insurance call scripts (FNOL, billing, status) are highly structured and AI-ready
- The BPO industry itself is disrupting ‚Äî AI-native entrants like Glima can leapfrog legacy BPO infrastructure
- Small carriers can't afford $150M AI platforms (Glia) but desperately need the cost savings

## 2. Segment Analysis

### 2.1 Personal Auto Insurance

**Market Size**
- US direct premiums written: ~$300B+ (2024, State Farm, Progressive, Berkshire/GEICO, Allstate, USAA dominate with 63.59% market share)
- Estimated call center spend: **$2-4B/year** (industry estimates ~1-2% of premiums on call center operations)

**Call Center Usage**
- FNOL intake (every accident triggers a call)
- Claims status follow-up
- Billing disputes / payment processing
- Policy changes and renewals
- Glass/roadside claims intake

**Outsourcing Propensity: Medium-High**
Large carriers run in-house operations; regional carriers and MGAs (there are 2,000+ P&C carriers licensed in the US, of which ~500+ write meaningful auto) actively outsource FNOL and billing to specialists like ACTEC, Covenir, and Focus BPO.

**Market Fragmentation: Medium**
Top 5 control 63% of market. However, ~1,500 carriers write auto in some capacity ‚Äî the long tail is very fragmented and cost-sensitive. Erie Insurance, GAINSCO, Kemper, The General, Bristol West are mid-size regional targets.

**AI Automation Potential: High**
FNOL calls are structured intake processes ‚Äî accident date, location, vehicle info, description. Claims status is a lookup. Billing is a script. All three are excellent AI candidates with high containment rates.

**Overall Attractiveness: ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê**
Large market, but dominated by in-house shops at the top. Best opportunity at the regional carrier and MGA level.

### 2.2 Homeowners Insurance

**Market Size**

**Call Center Usage**

Homeowners insurers face catastrophic call surges (hurricanes, wildfires) that make 24/7 in-house staffing economically irrational. Most mid-size carriers outsource surge capacity. Covenir's "Call Surge Guarantee" is specifically built for this.

**Market Fragmentation: High**
Hundreds of carriers, especially in coastal/high-risk states where national carriers have retreated. Florida, Texas, California have spawned dozens of small specialty homeowners carriers (Citizens, Heritage, Universal P&C, HCI Group, etc.).

**AI Automation Potential: High**
FNOL intake for home claims (storm damage, water damage) is structured. Weather-triggered surges are predictable. AI can handle initial intake and triage before routing complex claims to adjusters.

**Overall Attractiveness: ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê**
Strong outsourcing demand, catastrophe-driven urgency, but complex claims still need humans. Great for FNOL intake automation.

### 2.3 Pet Insurance

**Market Size**

**AI Automation Potential: Very High**
Pet insurance calls are extremely structured. FNOL ("my dog needs surgery") follows a simple intake script. Claims status is a database lookup. Policy questions are FAQ-style. Voice AI can handle 70-80%+ of calls with high customer satisfaction ‚Äî pet owners just want quick answers.

**Overall Attractiveness: ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê**
Fast-growing, fragmented, no legacy infrastructure, founder-led companies open to new vendors, high call volume relative to team size, simple call scripts. **This is Glima's beachhead.**

## 4. Types of Operations Outsourced to Call Centers

### 4.1 FNOL ‚Äî First Notice of Loss

**What it is:** The first call a policyholder makes to report an accident, theft, or loss. For auto: "I just got into an accident." For home: "My basement flooded."

**Why it gets outsourced:** 24/7 coverage requirement, unpredictable surge volume, requires specialized training (empathy + data collection protocol). Most carriers can't staff this cost-effectively in-house.

**AI Automation Potential: High**
AI can gather structured incident data (date, location, parties involved, description) following a fixed intake script. Liberate claims 75% digital FNOL automation. Floatbot claims 80% of FNOL submissions automated.

**Commentary:** For voice AI, FNOL is the sweet spot. The call has a clear beginning, middle, and end. The information needed is structured. Emotional support can be scripted ("I understand this is stressful ‚Äî let me get your claim started right away"). ElevenLabs-quality voice makes this feel human.

**Voice AI Ready Today: YES** ‚Äî with proper escalation paths for complex situations.

---

### 4.2 Claims Status Follow-Up

**What it is:** Policyholders calling to check "Where is my claim? When will I get paid?" ‚Äî typically multiple calls per claim lifecycle.

**Why it gets outsourced:** High volume, low complexity. The caller wants one piece of information. Agents spend most of the call on hold looking up data.

**AI Automation Potential: High**
Pure lookup + communication. AI connects to claims management system, authenticates the policyholder, reads out claim status. No judgment needed.

**Commentary:** This may be the single highest ROI automation Glima can offer. It's the most frequent call type, the most repetitive, and the most amenable to automation. Every carrier that outsources this is paying $15-25/call for information retrieval.

**Voice AI Ready Today: YES** ‚Äî this should be the MVP feature.

---

### 4.3 Billing & Payments

**What it is:** Premium payment processing, payment plan setup, NSF call resolution, billing disputes.

**Why it gets outsourced:** Routine, rules-based, high volume. No claims expertise needed.

**AI Automation Potential: High**
Taking payments, reading back balance information, setting up autopay ‚Äî all AI-native. Integration with payment processors (Stripe, etc.) is straightforward.

**Commentary:** Voice AI excels here. "Your premium of $147 is due on March 15. Would you like to pay now with the card on file?" is a 60-second automated call.

**Voice AI Ready Today: YES**

---

### 4.4 Policy Renewals

**What it is:** Proactive outbound calls to remind policyholders about upcoming renewals, quote new rates, retain at-risk accounts.

**Why it gets outsourced:** High volume, seasonal, requires clear communication skills.

**AI Automation Potential: High**
Outbound AI calls for renewal reminders are a well-established use case. Quoting is more complex but can be scripted for simple personal lines.

**Commentary:** The outbound renewal call is an excellent AI use case ‚Äî scripted, outcome-measurable, and currently expensive at scale. This could be a second product module after inbound claims.

**Voice AI Ready Today: YES (for simple lines)**

---

### 4.5 New Customer Onboarding

**What it is:** Welcome calls after policy purchase, explaining coverage, setting up payment, collecting any remaining information.

**Why it gets outsourced:** High volume in growth phases, consistency is critical.

**AI Automation Potential: Medium-High**
Structured script, but requires some flexibility to answer questions. Works well with AI + escalation.

**Commentary:** Onboarding calls are relationship-building moments. AI can handle the structured parts (welcome, coverage summary, payment setup) and escalate to human for complex questions.

**Voice AI Ready Today: YES (with human escalation)**

---

### 4.6 Customer Service / FAQ

**What it is:** General "Where can I find my policy? What is my deductible? How do I add a driver?" questions.

**Why it gets outsourced:** Highly variable, 24/7 demand, difficult to staff efficiently.

**AI Automation Potential: High**
Most insurance FAQs have known answers. RAG-based AI on the policy document + carrier knowledge base can answer 80%+ of these calls.

**Commentary:** This is where LLM-backed voice AI shines. ElevenLabs' agent architecture supports tool use and knowledge retrieval. A carrier's FAQ + policy terms fed into the context window handles most calls.

**Voice AI Ready Today: YES**

---

### 4.7 Fraud Detection Intake

**What it is:** Red-flag flagging during FNOL or claims calls. Suspicious timing, inconsistent stories, prior claim history flags.

**Why it gets outsourced:** Requires both call handling and analysis. Specialized BPOs have scoring models.

**AI Automation Potential: Medium**
AI can flag linguistic inconsistencies, unusual claim patterns, and metadata anomalies. But final fraud determination requires human adjuster review. AI as a co-pilot for fraud detection is ready; AI as the decision-maker is not.

**Commentary:** Glima can build fraud flagging as a feature (sentiment analysis, inconsistency detection) rather than a standalone product.

**Voice AI Ready Today: PARTIALLY ‚Äî as a flag/alert system**

---

### 4.8 Roadside Assistance Dispatch

**What it is:** "I'm stuck on the highway, my tire is flat, I need a tow." Intake, GPS location, dispatch to nearest service provider.

**Why it gets outsourced:** 24/7, high volume, very structured ‚Äî all calls follow the same pattern.

**AI Automation Potential: Very High**
This is perhaps the most automatable call type in insurance. Location collection, service type selection, dispatch confirmation ‚Äî all fully scriptable. No empathy complexity of a serious accident claim.

**Commentary:** Several tech providers already offer this. Agero (largest roadside network) is moving toward automation. An AI that handles "I need a tow" end-to-end is entirely feasible today.

**Voice AI Ready Today: YES ‚Äî probably the highest containment rate of any call type**

## 5. Competitive Landscape

### 5.1 Traditional Insurance BPO / Call Center Outsourcers

**1. ACTEC (actec.com)**
- *Focus:* FNOL specialist ‚Äî one of the oldest and most respected FNOL outsourcers in the US
- *Description:* Handles FNOL intake, claims services, property loss services. Operates 365/24/7. Strong auto, homeowners, specialty lines focus.
- *Pricing:* Per-call or per-minute (estimated $15-35/call)
- *Weakness:* Human-only model, no AI offering. Loyal customer base but vulnerable to AI disruption.

**2. Covenir BPO (covenirbpo.com)**
- *Focus:* Onshore US insurance BPO ‚Äî FNOL, claims, print/mail
- *Description:* "100% US-based" positioning, Call Surge Guarantee, serves carriers and insurtechs. Has hybrid AI-human workflow product.
- *Pricing:* SLA-based contracts, per-call pricing
- *Weakness:* Still fundamentally a staffing model, AI bolted on rather than native.

**3. Confie BPO (confiebpo.com)**
- *Focus:* Personal lines insurance ‚Äî #1 in personal lines for 6 years
- *Description:* 200+ agents, handles claims, sales, service for auto and home insurance carriers
- *Pricing:* Per-agent FTE or per-transaction
- *Weakness:* Offshore-leaning, volume-focused, not tech-forward.

**4. Focus Insurance BPO (teamfocusins.com)**
- *Focus:* Full-stack BPO + technology for carriers and MGAs
- *Description:* Has both technology platform (policy admin) and BPO services. Has helped carriers launch from zero to 35,000 policies. Strong in small commercial and homeowners.
- *Pricing:* Bundled tech + BPO, enterprise contracts
- *Weakness:* Technology-heavy onboarding, less plug-and-play.

**5. Liveops (liveops.com)**
- *Focus:* Flexible, gig-model call center agents ‚Äî a talent marketplace for insurance calls
- *Description:* On-demand licensed insurance agents, handles FNOL and customer service peaks
- *Pricing:* Per-minute/per-call, flexible surge model

**6. CustomerServ (customerserv.com)**

**8. TeleDirect (teledirect.com)**
- *Focus:* Multi-industry call center with strong insurance vertical

**9. 1-800 Notify / Outsource Consultants**

- *Description:* Matches insurance carriers with optimal BPO partners ‚Äî not a direct operator

**10. Conduent / Genpact / Cognizant / EXL Service / WNS**
- *Focus:* Large enterprise insurance BPO
- *Description:* The Mordor Intelligence "major players" ‚Äî handle large carrier accounts (MetLife, Prudential, large health plans). Focus on back-office + enterprise call center. Not relevant to Glima's target market initially.
